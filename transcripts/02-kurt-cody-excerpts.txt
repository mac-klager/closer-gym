CONDENSED EXCERPTS — real discovery call (prospect from medical device sales), Closer: Kurt / Prospect: Cody
Focus: higher-income prospect — pain is time/ceiling/identity, not survival. Skeptical, analytical, asks counter-questions.

Kurt: Cody, what's going on man, you good?
Cody: Yeah, I'm good. Just got off work actually, so good timing.
Kurt: What is it you do?
Cody: I'm in medical device sales. So I'm basically driving between hospitals and clinics all day covering my territory.
Kurt: Nice. How long you been doing that?
Cody: Going on six years now.
Kurt: And how's it treating you?
Cody: It's... fine. The money's decent. It's just a grind, dude. Like a real grind.
Kurt: Define grind for me.
Cody: I'm in my car probably four hours a day. I'm on call for cases. If a surgery runs at 6am I'm there at 5:30. My comp plan changed twice in two years and both times it got worse, not better. So.

Kurt: What made you book this call though? Guy making decent money in device sales doesn't usually book a call off an Instagram ad.
Cody: Ha. Fair. Honestly? I've been following this space for a while. Like I get how sales works, obviously, it's what I do. And I keep seeing guys doing what I do but from a laptop, no territory, no driving, no being owned by a hospital schedule. And I'm like, what am I doing.
Kurt: How long have you been watching before you booked?
Cody: Probably... eight months? Something like that.
Kurt: Eight months. Why now?
Cody: [pause] We just found out my wife's pregnant. So. Things feel a little different now.
Kurt: Congrats man. First one?
Cody: First one, yeah. Thanks.
Kurt: So what does that change?
Cody: I mean, everything? The travel mostly. My dad traveled for work my whole childhood and I told myself I wasn't gonna do that. And here I am basically doing that.

Kurt: With your sales background, what's stopped you from just making the jump already?
Cody: Couple things. One, I don't know the online space — like I can sell a device to a surgeon because I know the product cold. I don't know these offers. Two, honestly, the space has a lot of garbage in it. No offense.
Kurt: None taken. What kind of garbage?
Cody: Like the guys flexing rented Lambos. The "six figures in 90 days" stuff. I sell for a living, I know what manufactured urgency looks like, so when I see it I just close the tab.
Kurt: So what would you actually need to see?
Cody: A real mechanism. Like show me where the deals come from, who's actually paying, what the ramp actually looks like. Real numbers, not screenshots.

Kurt: If this works the way it's worked for the guys we talked about — what does that look like for you 12 months from now?
Cody: Best case? I'm fully out of device sales before the baby's like, six months old. Working from home. Same income or better — I'm at about 140 all in right now, so it'd have to make sense financially, I'm not taking a step back with a kid coming.
Kurt: And worst case if you do nothing?
Cody: Worst case I'm doing exactly this in five years with a kid I see on weekends. Which is the thing I said I'd never do. So.
Kurt: You said that pretty fast.
Cody: Because I've thought about it a lot, man.

Cody: Okay, so what's the investment look like? And don't do the thing where you ask me what I think it costs.
Kurt: [laughs] Fair enough...
[after price]
Cody: Okay. That's in the range I figured. Here's my honest hesitation — it's not the money. We have it. It's that if I do this, I'm doing it for real, and I need to know the support is real when I hit a wall at week six. Because I will hit a wall. What does that actually look like?
Kurt: That's the right question to ask...
Cody: And the other thing is my wife. Not like a permission thing — we just don't drop five figures without both being in the room. That's just how we run things. So whatever happens today, that conversation happens tonight.
Kurt: Totally fair. What do you think she's going to say?
Cody: Honestly? She's been telling me to do something about this for a year. She'll probably say it's about time.
