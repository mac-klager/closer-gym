CONDENSED EXCERPTS — real discovery call (high ticket sales program), Closer: Kurt / Prospect: Kevin
Focus: prospect voice, pacing, how slowly truth comes out.

Kurt: Hey Kevin, can you hear me alright?
Kevin: Yeah, yeah I can hear you.
Kurt: Perfect. So just so I understand, what made you book this call in?
Kevin: Um, I mean... I've just been looking into different ways to make money online I guess. Saw the ad and it seemed pretty legit so figured I'd check it out.
Kurt: Okay, and when you say looking into different ways — how long has that been going on?
Kevin: Honestly like... a couple years probably. Off and on.
Kurt: A couple years. And what have you actually tried in that time?
Kevin: Uh, not a whole lot to be honest. I watched a lot of videos. I bought a course at one point but I didn't really... I didn't finish it.
Kurt: What course was that?
Kevin: It was like a dropshipping thing. It was fine, I just — life got busy, you know.

Kurt: So walk me through it — what do you do right now?
Kevin: I work at a warehouse. Been there about four years now.
Kurt: How is it?
Kevin: It's alright. I mean it pays the bills. It's just a lot of hours.
Kurt: How many hours we talking?
Kevin: Like 50, sometimes 60 if I pick up overtime. Which I usually do because, you know.
Kurt: Because?
Kevin: The money. Regular checks don't really cut it on their own.

Kurt: Let me ask you this — four years in, where does this go? Like if you stay on this track, what does year ten look like?
Kevin: ...Yeah I mean that's kind of the thing. There's guys there who've been there fifteen, twenty years and they're doing the same thing I'm doing. Maybe they're a lead or whatever, making like two dollars more an hour.
Kurt: How does that sit with you when you see that?
Kevin: Not great, honestly. Like no offense to them but... I don't want that to be me.
Kurt: Why not? What's wrong with it?
Kevin: I just feel like I'm meant for more than scanning boxes, man. I don't know. That sounds dumb.
Kurt: It doesn't sound dumb at all. Who else knows you feel that way?
Kevin: I mean... my girlfriend kind of knows. We don't really talk about it like that though.

Kurt: If money wasn't the issue — what would you actually want your life to look like?
Kevin: Honestly just like... not having to check my bank account before I buy groceries. Being able to take my girl somewhere nice. Nothing crazy. I'm not trying to buy a Lambo or whatever.
Kurt: What would be the first thing you'd do?
Kevin: First thing? Probably get out of my apartment. The area's not great. That'd be the first thing for sure.

Kurt: So real talk — what's stopped you? Two years of looking, one course you didn't finish. What's the real reason?
Kevin: I don't know man. I guess... I guess part of it is I don't want to waste money on something that doesn't work. Like everything out there says the same stuff and you don't know what's real.
Kurt: Fair. What would make something feel real to you?
Kevin: Like actual people getting actual results I guess. And someone actually showing you instead of just selling you a bunch of videos.

Kurt: If you keep doing exactly what you're doing now — nothing changes — where are you in a year?
Kevin: ...Same place. Probably exactly the same place.
Kurt: And how do you feel saying that out loud?
Kevin: Not good. [pause] Yeah, not good man.

Kevin: So what's like... what does this cost? Just so I know what we're talking about.
Kurt: I'll get to that, I promise — but it'd be a disservice to throw a number at you before you even know what it is. Fair?
Kevin: Yeah, fair, fair.

[later, after presentation]
Kevin: Okay yeah. That's... more than I thought honestly.
Kurt: What were you expecting?
Kevin: I don't know, like maybe a thousand?
Kurt: And what's the gap between a thousand and this mean for you — is it a value thing or a logistics thing?
Kevin: It's not that I don't think it's worth it. It's just, I don't have that sitting in my checking account, you know?
Kurt: Sure. If we figured out the logistics side and it actually made sense — is this something you want to do?
Kevin: I mean... yeah. Yeah, honestly. I'm tired of being where I'm at.
Kevin: Could I do like, half now or something? I get paid Friday too.
